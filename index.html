About the Author

Allama Sayyed Imdad Hussain Kazmi Al‑Mashhadi, son of Sayyed Abbas Ali Shah Kazmi Al‑Mashhadi, was born in November 1901 in Gujrat (Punjab). His grandfather, Mir Sayyed Ramzan Ali Kazmi Al‑Mashhadi, was a renowned pious and ascetic jurist. A sacred personality such as Mujtahid‑ul‑Asr Allama Sayyed Hashmat Ali Khairullahpuri (may Allah elevate his status) would visit him personally. He was widely respected in Lucknow and Amroha for his majalis‑khwani, earning him the title Sultan‑ul‑Zakireen.

His father, Sayyed Farzand Ali Kazmi, was a student of Allama Hakeem Sayyed Ghulam‑ul‑Hasnain Kishwari (may Allah elevate his status) and studied at Madrassa Aimania.

Allama Imdad received his early education in Wazirabad, then Gujranwala, and later Lahore. He passed the examinations of Munshi Fazil, Maulvi Fazil, and Adeeb Fazil with distinction from Punjab University, after which he completed his B.A. degree. The scholars of Jamia Nazmia Lucknow were so impressed by his academic excellence that they awarded him the honorary title “Shams‑ul‑Afazil”. He also passed the Gurmukhi examination and obtained a Giani certificate.

Despite being in government service, his dedication to serving the true religion remained his defining trait. His groundbreaking sermons in Jammu & Kashmir and Poonch are unforgettable. In the court of Poonch, he delivered several consecutive sermons under the presidency of Raja Jagat Dev Singh, the ruler of Poonch. Impressed by his eloquence and scholarship, the state conferred upon him the title “Abul Fazl Sani” and presented him with a robe of honour and a certificate. He also preached in Assam, Madras, Bengal, Central Provinces, and United Provinces. He holds numerous ijazaat from scholars of Iraq, Pakistan, and India.

His writings appeared in almost all major newspapers of undivided India. He served as the editor of the well‑known Ahl‑e‑Sunnat magazine “Sufi”. In addition to Punjabi and Urdu, he was proficient in English, Arabic, and Persian. His English poems and articles were published in London’s VICTORY magazine and Delhi’s POSTAL COMRADE. For the past five years, his academic and research work has been regularly featured in the monthly Ma'arif‑e‑Islam.

Works and Writings

His renowned book Tafsir bil‑Ra’y has received praise from scholars and learned individuals. His other works include:

  • Tahqeeq‑e‑Mahdi
  • Al‑Fatima
  • Barakat‑e‑Muharram bajawab Bid'at‑e‑Muharram
  • Tatbiq‑ush‑Shahadat
  • A'mal‑e‑Wajibiya
  • Mu'allim‑ul‑Islam

His work Akhlaq‑ul‑Ma'sumeen is his latest, currently under review. His monumental book Istiqlal‑e‑Haq‑e‑Azadari, comprising 1,500 pages, is ready for publication. It is an encyclopedia of the Azadari of Imam Hussain (peace be upon him).

His translation of the Holy Qur’an, with annotations from Tafsir‑ul‑Muttaqin, is being published by Idara Insaaf Publishing Company, Lahore.

Due to his selfless service and profound erudition, he has been unanimously elected President of Idara Ma'arif‑e‑Islam for the past four consecutive years, and he also serves as the Honorary Chief Supervisor of the monthly Ma'arif‑e‑Islam.

In this era of widespread confusion and disarray, he stands out as a uniquely qualified leader—distinguished by his piety, sincerity, impartiality, and scholarly excellence.

— From the Author’s Introduction to The Morals of the Infallibles, by Yahya Ali Shah Al‑Naqvi Al‑Bukhari, General Secretary, Idara Ma'arif‑e‑Islam, Lahore.
