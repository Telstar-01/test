Welcome

This website represents the authoritative family-curated Digital Collection, preserving the intellectual, historical, and cultural legacy of Allama Sayyid Imdad Hussain Kazmi al-Mashhadi. It serves as the modern public interface for his works, preserved permanently on the Internet Archive and where applicable, assigned DOIs via Zenodo.

The digitised manuscripts, transcriptions, edited texts, translations, annotations, introductions, commentary, and the structure and presentation of this collection constitute new creative works. The family continues to maintain this collection as a non commercial, cultural and scholarly legacy of the extant works of the author. The aim is to ensure accurate preservation, responsible access, and respect for the author’s contributions to Islamic scholarship and Urdu literature.

Researchers, publishers, and readers are welcome to contact the estate for use of the digitised editions, translations, annotations, or curated materials presented on this website.

Contributions and Collaboration

The estate welcomes information from individuals, families, institutions, or collectors who may hold original publications, manuscripts, letters, or other materials relating to the work of Allama Sayyid Imdad Hussain Kazmi. Such contributions assist in completing the historical record and in preserving the author’s intellectual legacy for future scholarship.

Holders of printed works, private correspondence, unpublished writings, or early editions are invited to make contact with the estate. Digital scans, bibliographic details, provenance information, or notices of surviving materials are all of value, and may help identify items not yet represented in the public record.

Where contributors wish to share material, the estate will provide guidance on rights, permissions, and the conditions under which items may be digitised or made accessible. Contributors retain ownership of their originals unless formally transferred, and all materials are handled in accordance with archival standards and applicable copyright law.

To discuss a potential contribution or to notify us of surviving material, please contact the estate. All enquiries are acknowledged, and collaboration with libraries, private holders, and community members is welcomed.
