Adapted from Yahya Ali Shah Naqvi al-Bukhari (General Secretary, Idara Ma'arif-e-Islam, Lahore), published in Akhlaq-ul-Ma'sumeen ‘The Morals of the Infallibles’ by Allama Sayyid Imdad Hussain Kazmi al-Mashhadi, 1960

The esteemed author, Allama Sayyid Imdad Hussain Kazmi al-Mashhadi, son of Sayyid Abbas Ali Shah Kazmi al-Mashhadi, was born in November 1901 in Gujrat (Punjab) and passed away in September 1975. He is buried in the Kadhimiya Cemetery near his family home in the Noorpur Sayyidan area of the city.

His late grandfather, Mir Sayyid Ramzan Ali Kazmi al-Mashhadi, was a highly pious, ascetic jurist of his time, to the extent that he used to be visited by Mujtahid-ul-Asr Allama Sayyid Hashmat Ali Khairullahpuri, 1858-1943 (may Allah elevate his status) an Indian Shia mujtahid whose wide travels, advanced studies in Lucknow, Iraq, Iran, and Turkey, and long teaching career made him one of the most respected scholarly authorities of his era. Mir Sayyid Ramzan Ali Kazmi was very famous and popular among the Sayyids and believers of Lucknow and Amroha due to his majalis-khwani (recitation at religious gatherings). He was known as Sultan-ul-Zakireen (King of Reciters) and spent most of his life there.

His paternal uncle, Sayyid Farzand Ali Kazmi, was among the students of Allama Hakeem Sayyid Ghulam-ul-Hasnain Kanturi, c.1832-1919 (may Allah elevate his status), a 19th‑century Lucknow‑based polymath whose scholarship spanned classical Islamic sciences, philosophy, and medicine, and whose writings — especially Intesār‑ul‑Islām - shaped modern Urdu Islamic thought. He received education at his Madressa Imania.

Allama Imdad received his initial education in Wazirabad, then Gujranwala, and later Lahore. After passing the examinations of Munshi Fazil, Maulvi Fazil, and Adeeb Fazil with high distinction from Punjab University, graduating with a B.A. The scholars of Jamia Nazmia Lucknow (founded in 1890 by Ayatullah Sayyid Najmul Hasan), impressed by his academic prowess, honorarily presented him with a written certificate of "Shams-ul-Afazil" (Sun of the Virtuous). He also passed the Gurmukhi examination and obtained a Giani certificate.

Despite being in government service, serving religion was his vocation, including groundbreaking sermons in the regions of Jammu & Kashmir and Poonch. He delivered several sermons under the presidency of Raja Jagat Dev Singh, the ruler of Poonch (1928-40). Impressed by these, the state bestowed upon him the title of "Abul Fazl Sani" (The Second Abul Fazl) and presented him with a robe of honour along with a certificate. While preaching in Poonch, he remained a royal guest. Besides this, he also continued to preach religion in Assam, Madras, Bengal, C.P. (Central Provinces), and U.P. (United Provinces). He also possesses numerous ijazaat (authorizations/licenses) from scholars of Iraq, Pakistan, and India, which attest to his profound scholarship.

His literary works have appeared across newspapers of India. He also served as the editor of the famous Ahl-e-Sunnat magazine "Sufi". Besides his mother tongues of Punjabi and Urdu, he was proficient in English, Arabic, and Persian. His English poems and articles have been published in London's famous magazine 'Victory' and Delhi's English magazine 'Postal Comrade', and in the late 1950s, Allama Imdad’s high-calibre academic and research material was presented in the monthly Ma'arif-e-Islam.

Idara Ma'arif-e-Islam Publishers (Lahore), Diaspora & International Chapters:

Idara Ma'arif-e-Islam was established during the 1950s as a Shia scholarly institution and publisher in Lahore. Allama Imdad Kazmi became a prominent member of the organisaiton, serving as chief supervisor of its monthly journal, Ma'arif-e-Islam. Influenced by the scholarly output and thought leadership emanating from Lahore across a wide array of subjects, new international chapters of the institution were established in England and East Pakistan.

Volume 7, Issue 4 of the journal (Page 8, July 1961) mentions a report from the England Chapter of Idara Ma'arif-e-Islam to the President of the Central Office in Lahore. The Birmingham secretary of Idara Ma'arif‑e‑Islam reports that, for what appears to be the first time in Britain, full Muharram majalis and mourning commemorations were observed in 1961, drawing more than forty committed believers, visitors from other cities, and widespread local attention. The majalis series was addressed daily by Nazir Hussain and Fida Hussain (who had been touring England with the party of the famous artist Muhammad Alam Lohar) and recited elegies (Marsiyas).

Alam Lohar’s own contribution to Karbala‑related devotional culture lies in his powerful folk renditions of the Dāstān‑e‑Karbala and other narrative pieces that retell the tragedy of Imam Husayn in a Punjabi idiom. Though not a classical noha‑khwan, he brought the emotional cadence of Muharram lamentation into mainstream folk performance, using vaars, qissas, and his signature vocal style to make the story of Karbala accessible to rural and working‑class audiences across Pakistan and, later, the diaspora. His recordings and touring performances helped familiarise broad Punjabi audiences with the themes of sacrifice, loyalty, and mourning associated with Ashura, and his troupe often included marsiya and noha reciters who reinforced this devotional atmosphere.

Volume 7, Issue 4 of the journal (Page 10, July 1961) contains a further report in which The East Pakistan secretary - writing from Tejgaon, Dhaka - mentions that during Muharram, the Idara’s Bengali language booklet Aqaid‑e‑Shia (Shia Beliefs) was widely distributed free of charge in Dhaka’s majalis, mosques, madrasas, and neighbourhoods. The booklet clarified Jafari beliefs, corrected long‑standing public misunderstandings about Shi‘ism, and included concise information on the birth and martyrdom dates of the Fourteen Infallibles and mention of Ahl al-Sunnah wal-Jama'ah scholars and contemporaries of their eras.

Major Works and Writings:

Allama Imdad's Urdu translation of the Holy Quran with annotations in his Tafsir-ul-Muttaqeen is an excellent treasure of academic research, published by Insaf Publishers Lahore with great care.

His renowned book, Tafsir bil-Ra'y, has received tribute and praise from scholars and learned individuals. Tahqeeq-e-Mahdi, Al-Fatima, Barakat-e-Muharram bajawab Bid'at-e-Muharram, Tatbiq-ush-Shahadat, A'mal-e-Wajibiya, and Mu'allim-ul-Islam are among his works. Akhlaq-ul-Ma'sumeen was among his later works. Istiqlal-e-Haq-e-Azadari, comprising 1500 pages, is available for publication. It is an encyclopedia of the Azadari of Imam Hussain (peace be upon him), comprehensively addressing all aspects of Azadari and their reasoning.

Due to his selfless services and profound erudition, he was unanimously elected as the President of Idara Ma'arif-e-Islam (Lahore) for four consecutive years, and was also the Honorary Chief Supervisor of the monthly Ma'arif-e-Islam.
