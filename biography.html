[Adapted from an introduction to the author by Yahya Ali Shah Al-Naqvi Al-Bukhari. General Secretary, Idara Ma'arif-e-Islam, Lahore, contained in Akhlaq-ul-Ma'sumeen ‘The Morals of the Infallibles’ by Allama Sayyed Imdad Hussain Kazmi al-Mashhadi, 1960]

The esteemed author, Allama Sayyed Imdad Hussain Kazmi al-Mashhadi, son of Sayyed Abbas Ali Shah Kazmi al-Mashhadi, was born in November 1901 in Gujrat (Punjab) and passed away in September 1975.

His late grandfather, Mir Sayyed Ramzan Ali Kazmi al-Mashhadi, was a highly pious, ascetic jurist of his time, to the extent that a sacred personality like Mujtahid-ul-Asr Allama Sayyed Hashmat Ali Khairullahpuri (may Allah elevate his status) used to visit him. He was very famous and popular among the Sayyeds and believers of Lucknow and Amroha due to his majalis-khwani (recitation of religious gatherings). He was known as Sultan-ul-Zakireen (King of Reciters) and spent most of his life there.

His elder paternal uncle, Sayyed Farzand Ali Kazmi, was among the students of Allama Hakeem Sayyed Ghulam-ul-Hasnain Kanturi (may Allah elevate his status). He received education at his Madrassa Aimania.

Allama Imdad received his initial education in Wazirabad, then Gujranwala, and later Lahore. After passing the examinations of Munshi Fazil, Maulvi Fazil, and Adeeb Fazil with high distinction from Punjab University, he completed his B.A. and became a graduate. The scholars of Jamia Nazmia Lucknow (the oldest Shia religious institution in India, founded in 1890 by Ayatullah Sayyed Najmul Hasan), impressed by his academic prowess, honorarily presented him with a written certificate of "Shams-ul-Afazil" (Sun of the Virtuous). He also passed the Gurmukhi examination and obtained a Giani certificate.

Despite being in government service, serving the true religion was his distinguishing feature. His groundbreaking sermons in the regions of Jammu & Kashmir and Poonch are unforgettable. Especially in the court of Poonch, he delivered several continuous sermons under the presidency of Raja Jagat Dev Singh, the ruler of Poonch. Impressed by these, the state bestowed upon him the title of "Abul Fazl Sani" (The Second Abul Fazl) and presented him with a robe of honour along with a certificate. For as long as he preached in Poonch, he remained as a royal guest. Besides this, he also continued to preach religion in Assam, Madras, Bengal, C.P. (Central Provinces), and U.P. (United Provinces). He also possesses numerous ijazaat (authorizations/licenses) from scholars of Iraq, Pakistan, and India, which attest to his profound scholarship.

His literary works have appeared in almost all newspapers of undivided India. He also served as the editor of the famous Ahl-e-Sunnat magazine "Sufi". Besides his mother tongues of Punjabi and Urdu, he was proficient in English, Arabic, and Persian. His English poems and articles have been published in London's famous magazine 'Victory' and Delhi's English magazine 'Postal Comrade', and in the late 1950s, Allama Imdad’s high-calibre academic and research material was presented in the monthly Ma'arif-e-Islam.

Works and Writings:

His renowned book, Tafsir bil-Ra'y, has received tribute and praise from scholars of the community and other learned individuals. Additionally, Tahqeeq-e-Mahdi, Al-Fatima, Barakat-e-Muharram bajawab Bid'at-e-Muharram (Blessings of Muharram in response to Innovations of Muharram), Tatbiq-ush-Shahadat, A'mal-e-Wajibiya, and Mu'allim-ul-Islam are also among his works. Akhlaq-ul-Ma'sumeen was among his latest latest works. Istiqlal-e-Haq-e-Azadari, comprising 1500 pages, is available for publication. It is virtually an encyclopedia of the Azadari of Imam Hussain (peace be upon him). It is comprehensive, and there is no aspect of Azadari whose justification is not present in it in every way.

Allama Imdad's translation of the Holy Quran with annotations from Tafsir-ul-Muttaqin is an excellent treasure of academic research, published by Insaaf Publishers Lahore with great care.

Due to his selfless services and profound erudition, he was unanimously elected as the President of Idara Ma'arif-e-Islam for four consecutive years, and was also the Honorary Chief Supervisor of the monthly Ma'arif-e-Islam.

In an era, when the nation was in a state of extreme disarray, he was the most suitable personality to take the reins of the nation due to his piety, sincerity, impartiality, and academic capability and importance.
