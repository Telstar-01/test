The literary, scholarly, and manuscript works of Allama Sayyed Imdad Hussain Kazmi al‑Mashhadi (d. 21 September 1975, Pakistan) were protected under the Copyright Ordinance of Pakistan (1962), which grants copyright protection for the lifetime of the author plus fifty (50) years. In accordance with this law, copyright in the author’s works expired on 31 December 2025, and the original texts entered the public domain in Pakistan on 1 January 2026.

The expiration of copyright applies to the author’s original writings only. The following remain protected:

  1. Moral Rights: The author’s right to be identified as the creator of his works, and the right to object to distortion, mutilation, or misrepresentation, continue to apply. These rights are recognised under international norms and remain important for scholarly integrity.
  2. Family‑Curated Editions and Digital Materials: The digitised manuscripts, transcriptions, edited texts, translations, annotations, introductions, commentary, and the structure and presentation of this archive constitute new creative works and remain protected by copyright held by the family.
  3. Official Digital Archive Identity: This website represents the authoritative family‑curated archive, preserving the intellectual, historical, and cultural legacy of Allama Sayyed Imdad Hussain Kazmi al‑Mashhadi. While the original texts are now public domain, the curated editions and contextual materials presented here remain the intellectual property of the Estate.
  4. Stewardship Purpose: The family continues to maintain this archive as a non‑commercial cultural and scholarly legacy, ensuring accurate preservation, responsible access, and respect for the author’s contributions to Islamic scholarship and Urdu literature.
